St. Pauls Cathedral is one of the most visited sites in London by travelers. A must see for those of the catholic faith and those looking to visit landmarks that are rich with historical evets. Since completion in 1710, the cathedral has survived terrorist attack attempts by suffragettes and war damage.

Located at the Palace of Westminister, Big Ben is one of the United Kingdoms tallest clock towers at 316 feet tall. While visitors aren't current allowed in the building to see it's inner wokings, they can still admire it's beauty from one of the many tour buses that drive by daily.

London's Tower Bridge is not to be confused with the London bridge. A remarkable landmark in London and allows for both cars and pedestrians to utilize it for crossing the river. It's towers also feature exhibition rooms where visitors can view the steam engines that once powered the lifts to allow ships to cross underneath.

More to see...

  • Tower of London

    The infamous Tower of London was built in 1078, with multiple expansions continuing until 1399. While it was originally built as a palace for superior ranking individuals during the medieval period it is most known for its use during the Tudor reign as a place to keep prisoners before their judgement of execution was to be carried out. Today, the property spans approximately 18 acres.

  • Victoria and Albert Museum

    This massive museum of arts contains nearly 150 galleries that showcases over two-million items from all across the globe collected over the past 5000 years. Spanning 12-acres, this museum is an all-day adventure for the art enthusiast.

  • The London Eye

    Possibly the most popular, modern tourist attraction in all of the United Kingdom, The London Eye offers a view of London that can't be found anywhere else. This particular ferris wheel hosts millions of visitors every single year and is worth the wait.
